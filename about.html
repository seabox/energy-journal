Energy & Mind Journal

About this app

A personal daily journal for tracking energy, fatigue, and the habits that shape how your brain and body feel — grounded in established health frameworks.

What is this?

Energy Journal is a private, browser-based tool for logging how you feel each day and identifying patterns over time. It combines fatigue and sleep tracking with the Healthy Mind Platter framework to give you a holistic picture of your mental and physical wellbeing.

Your data stays with you — stored in your browser or optionally synced to your own cloud storage. Nothing is sent to any server run by this app.

Field guide

FieldWhat it means
Date The calendar date the entry is for. Only one entry per date is kept — saving on an existing date updates it.
Fatigue (0–10) Your subjective fatigue level. 0 = no fatigue at all; 10 = extreme, debilitating exhaustion. This is the primary metric the Insights section analyses.
Minutes Exercised Total minutes of physical activity. The WHO recommends at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic activity per week for adults. Filling this in auto-sets the Physical platter slider.
Exercise Type Free text — walk, run, swim, yoga, etc. Useful for spotting which activities correlate with better energy.
Steps Daily step count. A NIH study found adults taking 8,000 or more steps per day had significantly reduced all-cause mortality risk.
Day Nap Whether you napped during the day. Useful context when sleep quality is low or fatigue is unusually high or low.
Mood Awareness (0–10) How present and self-aware you felt. 0 = no self-awareness; 10 = fully in the moment, clearly aware of your emotions and internal state. Based on mindfulness concepts in Dr Dan Siegel’s mindsight work.
Mood A free-text label for how you felt — e.g. “anxious”, “calm”, “irritable”. Shown in the history table for quick review.
Notes Anything worth remembering about the day — stressors, illness, travel, significant events. The more specific, the more useful the AI deep-dive becomes.

The Healthy Mind Platter

Developed by Dr Dan Siegel (Clinical Professor of Psychiatry, UCLA) and David Rock (Director, NeuroLeadership Institute) in 2011. It describes seven daily mental activities that together promote integration and wellbeing in the brain — analogous to the way a balanced food plate covers nutritional needs.

Official Healthy Mind Platter resource →

Platter areaWhat it captures
Sleep Quality How refreshing your sleep was. Sleep is the brain’s primary restoration mechanism — it clears metabolic waste, consolidates memory, and regulates mood. The Sleep Foundation recommends 7–9 hours for most adults.
Focus time Goal-oriented, deep concentration time. Closely focusing on challenging tasks forms strong neural connections that build cognitive capacity over time.
Playtime Spontaneous, creative, or novel activity — anything playful and unstructured. Helps the brain form new connections and enhances the ability to innovate and adapt.
Connecting Time with other people (ideally face-to-face) or appreciating your connection to the natural world. Social connection activates brain circuits that promote safety and emotional regulation.
Physical (Exercise) Aerobic physical movement. Exercise releases BDNF (brain-derived neurotrophic factor), improving mood, focus, and long-term cognitive health. Auto-populated from Minutes Exercised.
Reflection Internal reflection — journalling, meditation, prayer, or quiet contemplative time. Builds capacity for emotional balance and self-awareness by engaging the medial prefrontal cortex.
Downtime Non-focused, unscheduled rest — mind-wandering, daydreaming, or simply relaxing without a goal. Allows the brain’s default mode network to restore mental clarity and creativity.
Nutrition Quality and consistency of eating. Good nutrition supports stable blood sugar, neurotransmitter production, and sustained energy. 0 = very poor; 10 = well-balanced and consistent.

Insights & AI deep-dive

The Fatigue Insights panel analyses your last 7 days of entries to surface:

  • Your average fatigue level and whether it is trending up or down vs the prior week
  • Your average sleep quality and how it relates to your fatigue levels
  • The Healthy Mind Platter areas you have invested in least over the last 3 days, with a practical suggestion for each

The Deeper insights via AI button compiles your last 21 days into a structured prompt and copies it to your clipboard, then opens Claude AI. You paste the prompt yourself — no data is sent automatically.

Technical requirements & features

  • Browser storage — entries are always saved to localStorage as a fallback, so the app works fully offline.
  • Cloud sync — OneDrive — connect a personal Microsoft account. Requires an Azure app registration with the Files.ReadWrite.AppFolder permission. Data is stored in your own OneDrive app folder, invisible to other apps.
  • Cloud sync — Google Drive — connect a Google account. Requires a Google Cloud project with the Drive API enabled and an OAuth2 client ID. Data is stored in your Drive’s private app-data folder (not visible in the Drive UI).
  • Change storage — the “Change storage” link beneath the header lets you switch providers at any time. Your local entries are preserved.
  • Dark / light mode — toggle with the ☀️ / 🌙 button in the header. Your preference is saved to localStorage.
  • CSV export — download all entries as a spreadsheet-compatible CSV from the Recent Entries section.
  • CSV import — import a previously exported CSV to restore or merge entries across devices.
  • Missing days indicator — orange badges appear above the history table for any of the last 7 days with no entry. Tap one to pre-fill that date.
  • Progressive Web App (PWA) — can be installed to your home screen on iOS/Android or as a desktop app via Chrome. Works offline once loaded.
  • No account required — this app has no server, no login, and no analytics. Cloud sync is entirely optional and uses only your own accounts.
